Embedding Ourselves in the Customer Journey

Understanding the customer’s job-to-be-done / instant fulfilment / staff empowerment


The job-to-be-done (JTBD) is a simple, yet revolutionary concept that lets us think beyond simple understanding of customer needs.  Understanding the customer’s JTBD helps us focus on the customer’s real intention of what they want to accomplish, hence building emotional connections as well as fulfilling functional needs.  JTBD is not a product or service or specific solution; rather it is the ‘unspoken’ reason or purpose which motivates customers to buy a product or service.  JTBD is at the heart of any customer activity!

Let’s use the example of credit cards.  When the customer uses the credit card (let’s call him Thomas), the functional need Thomas has is to pay for the goods or services he just purchased.  On many occasions though, a particular credit card is used not only to facilitate the payment but also to create an impression of achievement and/or status. Thomas will likely use a DBS Insignia, to fulfil both the functional need (make the payment) as well as the emotional need (to demonstrate achievement/ status). When we created the DBS Insignia card, to have a successful product we had to take care of both needs.

Now imagine that Thomas lost his wallet.  He calls the DBS hotline to report the loss and to cancel the cards to prevent any unauthorized transactions.  That’s clearly a functional purpose.  At a deeper, emotional level though, Thomas wants to get his life back to normal as quickly as possible - he both wants and needs to get everything in his wallet replaced.

If we continually think of the end-to-end customer journey (CJ) and JTBD, we can use it to build intuitive, straight-through processing, that deliver instant fulfillment. Now that will be difficult for our competitors to copy or replicate!

Think about this

What should we change that would help to simplify our products and services and hence help us deliver instantfulfillment and joyful experiences? What can we do to empower our staff so that they can own and solve problems instantly, without the need for handovers, and therefore, delays in resolving issues?
