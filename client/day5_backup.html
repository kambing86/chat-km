Empowerment begins with me

Empowering oneself is an attitude and the strongest source of that can be found in one place - ourselves.  Don’t get me wrong, there are many things within your scope of work that may not necessarily be within your realm of influence. But you’ll find that oftentimes, there is a good 15 percent  that you have the power to change and to transform those choices into desired actions and outcomes.

Empowerment is really a two-step process – it starts with taking ownership and then taking action, not just withinyour immediate area of responsibility but also within your sphere of influence. It’s about empowering oneself and empowering others.

I remember a few years ago, DBS had an issue with the administration of student loans.  I was in CB operations then and my staff who had some experience with the portfolio, came to me with a detailed analysis of the situation.  We knew it was an area where we could offer our expertise.  So we discussed with the business and deployed three of our staff and worked with them over a period of four months to resolve the issues.  Taking action especially when it is beyond our immediate area of responsibility is the hallmark of empowering oneself. Simply ask the question “How can I be of help to you?”.  Imagine how powerful that is if every one of us goes into a meeting with this attitude.

Empowering others on the other hand, is simply an act of increasing the space for others to make sensible decisions. As the person empowering others, one has to learn to let go.  Give simple instructions and broad parameters so that the person can carry forward that responsibility with assurance.  Otherwise, you’ll just get in the way of their experimentation and learning.  With success, comes confidence.  How can we apply this at work?  Start simple.  Push decision making one level down.

My experience with empowerment is that when you let people decide the best way to achieve an outcome with some simple guidance; they tend to amaze you with their creativity.  That’s the energy you will create when you empower others.

What specific actions can we commit to in order to empower ourselves and/or our teams?

(adapted from Jimmy Ng, DBS Housewarming editorial post)
