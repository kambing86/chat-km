Embracing the Digital World

What was acceptable 5 years ago is no longer acceptable – things move too fast!

How many times did you check your mobile phone today? I probably glanced at it more than 200 times. Research shows we spend more time with our phones than our PCs and most of it on various apps. While entertainment and games take up a chunk of that time, e-commerce and other convenience-related uses have gone up tremendously.

Companies have to adapt fast or risk going defunct

For us, the competition isn’t just from other banks, but from technology firms like Google, Apple, Facebook, WeChat, etc. These companies aren’t restricted by the number of physical outlets needed to distribute their products. These companies have minimal brick and mortar investments and instead built platforms that consumers want to engage with, regardless of physical location, device, age, or time of day. The consumer is seeking greater convenience and constantly on the look-out for better service standards.

Where are we on the digital journey?

We have committed close to S$1 billion to help us become more nimble and leverage adaptive technology that allows us to scale fast and effectively. Human centred design: We need to follow our customers, not just through their day, or on special occasions, but through their lives. And we have created more collaborative environments in order to encourage creative ways of working and embrace a digital mindset.

What’s down the road?

We need to constantly scan the landscape, embrace possibilities and tools of the future together with partners. Leverage technology, data, design, experimentation and embrace new skill sets to shape the future of banking. We need to do more – we need to think of new business models built on digital and how we can serve customers instantly.

Think about this

What does embracing digital mean to you?

How can we leverage digital to engage customers on more meaningful levels and increase our speed to market?
